Context

Workday’s mobile app needed animations to make it feel more polished. I chose FramerJS over Principle to create these animations because the handoff to development would be easier. I worked with a software developer intern to implement some of these animations onto the iOS app. It’s important to note that I completed this work with a visual design perspective rather than my typical human centered design process.

Delightful Empty States

My teammate wanted to include an empty state animation that appeared when the user scrolled all the way down on a page or when there wasn’t enough content to fill an entire page. He was a visual designer and felt that this addition would be more pleasant than a blank bottom segment. He worked on the vectors and I created the animations.

Learnings

Overall I really enjoyed using FramerJS and I’m excited that this experience opened up the possibility for me to create more robust and dynamic prototypes. I’m excited that I can essentially translate any concept into an interactive prototype to communicate my ideas to others more clearly. This experience with animations taught me that being subtle is prefered because complex animations can take away from the experience. Consistency is also essential to good motion design.

While I was working on animations, I also worked on redesigning Workday's universal conclusion interface to better support their 50,000 tasks.
